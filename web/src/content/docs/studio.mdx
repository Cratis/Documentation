## Why a control room

An event-sourced system is honest by design — nothing is overwritten, everything that happened is
still there. But that truth lives in a stream of events, not a table you can eyeball. When something
looks off in a read model, you want to answer "*which events produced this, and when?*" without writing
a query.

That's what Studio is for: a visual, real-time view of your system that speaks events, projections, and
read models — not rows and columns.

## What Studio will do

<div class="sl-card-grid">
    <SimpleCard icon="📡" title="See every event live">
        Watch events land in the log in real time, filtered by event source, type, or namespace —
        the heartbeat of your system, as it happens.
    </SimpleCard>
    <SimpleCard icon="🧮" title="Inspect & manage projections">
        See which projections are running, where they are in the stream, and replay or rewind one when
        you change how a read model is built.
    </SimpleCard>
    <SimpleCard icon="⏪" title="Replay & rewind the log">
        Roll an observer back to a sequence number and let it rebuild — the superpower event sourcing
        gives you, with a button instead of a script.
    </SimpleCard>
    <SimpleCard icon="🔎" title="Explore read models">
        Browse the read models your projections produce and trace any value back to the events that
        shaped it.
    </SimpleCard>
    <SimpleCard icon="🩺" title="Diagnose what's stuck">
        Find a paused partition or a failing reactor, see why it stopped, and get it moving again.
    </SimpleCard>
    <SimpleCard icon="🧭" title="Model collaboratively">
        Shape your events and slices as a team — event modeling as a first-class, shared activity.
    </SimpleCard>
</div>
